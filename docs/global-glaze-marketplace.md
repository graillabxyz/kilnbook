# Global Glaze Marketplace

Flux and Fire can become a global glaze database and seller network without losing its social-first core. A marketplace listing should always be attached to a real glaze profile, not a loose product tile. That keeps commerce connected to results, firings, clay bodies, application notes, safety notes, and seller credibility.

## Product Position

- The home feed remains social-first.
- Explore becomes both a result database and a global glaze discovery surface.
- Seller profiles can list glazes from their own public profiles.
- A listed glaze can be a digital recipe, dry mix, wet glaze, sample tile, or consultation.
- Recipe visibility remains separate from sale visibility. A seller can sell a dry mix while keeping the underlying recipe private.
- Listings should show evidence: fired results, clay bodies, firing ranges, cone, atmosphere, safety notes, and seller context.

## Seller Profile Model

Any profile can identify as an artist, studio, researcher, supplier, collective, educator, or custom identity. Selling should not require studio language, but a seller needs public profile metadata:

- Seller name
- Seller location or shipping origin
- Global or regional availability
- Shop URL
- Shop policies
- Business/contact links when available
- Public glaze listings

Business tier can unlock richer seller tooling, but the UX should still feel like a profile first, not a storefront first.

## Glaze Listing Model

Each sellable glaze profile can include:

- Marketplace enabled
- Listing formats: digital recipe, dry mix, wet glaze, sample tile, consultation
- Price label
- Shop URL
- Ships from
- Global shipping flag
- Inventory status: in stock, made to order, limited, sold out
- Fulfillment notes
- Safety disclosure

The listing should not expose private recipe ingredients unless the recipe version visibility is public.

## Trust And Safety

- Sellers are responsible for accurate safety, food-safety, and shipping disclosures.
- Flux and Fire should distinguish community evidence from seller claims.
- Public result history should help buyers evaluate how a glaze behaves across clay bodies and firings.
- The app should avoid making food-safety guarantees on behalf of sellers.
- Future checkout must include seller terms, tax/shipping handling, refunds, and dispute flows.

## Future Commerce Infrastructure

The first implementation can link out to seller shops. Later phases can add:

- Stripe Connect seller onboarding
- In-app checkout
- SKU variants for batch size and format
- Shipping zones and rates
- Seller verification
- Reviews tied to real result groups
- Marketplace moderation and reporting
- Digital recipe licensing and download access

The important rule: commerce grows from credible glaze records and public result evidence.
